Introduction to Safeguarding


Definition: Safeguarding refers to the measures taken to protect the health, well-being, and human rights of individuals, particularly children and vulnerable adults, to ensure they live free from abuse, harm, and neglect.

Recognizing Signs of Abuse



Physical Abuse:Explain what physical abuse is and provide examples of indicators, such as unexplained injuries, bruises, or fearfulness around adults.

Emotional Abuse:Describe emotional abuse and its signs, including anxiety, depression, and withdrawal.

Sexual Abuse:Define sexual abuse and its indicators, such as inappropriate sexual behavior or knowledge, and physical symptoms like pain or bruising in the genital area.

Neglect:Define sexual abuse and its indicators, such as inappropriate sexual behavior or knowledge, and physical symptoms like pain or bruising in the genital area.

Neglect:Outline what constitutes neglect and its signs, including poor hygiene, malnutrition, and a lack of appropriate clothing or medical care.
